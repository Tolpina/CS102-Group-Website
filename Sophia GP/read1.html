Early Life

Since childhood I always aspired to be a dictator, and at a grander scale, being the sole cause of world domination. My early flame of interest was the catalyst that created my ambition surrounding my president candidacy. Growing up in rural Indiana I had a busy and active childhood, and was instilled with the importance of hard work and fighting for your aspirations. I was raised in a bustling household with 12 siblings, my parents and 7 estranged cousins. My father started a porcupine farmstead focussed on the raising/rehabilitation and and milking of porcupines. I had to work in the porcupine stables everyday from daybreak to dawn and every time I was pierced by by the sharp and unforgiving quills, I promised myself that I would leave Indiana and focus my hardened morals and foundation of hard work to something important, and of a national scale. On the day of my 18th birthday I left behind my humble life and the rolling hills and humid breeze of indiana and set off to the great unknown. I was not unbeknown to the struggles I would encounter in this seemingly new world, I had prepared for this moment since it was 8 years old, every moment I wasn't in the stables I was researching the world and the subject of politics in order to prepare myself for my impending journey. I stayed up reading book after book next top the widow in my room using the moonlight to reveal the words until I had read every book in or school library and then the public library and anywhere else I could find. I spent my days working, my nights reading and any other bit of free time practicing my debating skills with my family. In 9th grade me and my debate team won Indiana's statewide debate contest and that was the first time my family realized my potential for a life in politics. My dad allowed me to volunteer as a student ta at my highschool instead of coming home to work on the farm, and when it was time for me to leave at age 18, they were sad but affirmed in my skills and were able to let me go. My upbringing allowed for me to find out what kind of person I was going to be and instilled in me the power in self-sustaining your thirst for knowledge and success.
